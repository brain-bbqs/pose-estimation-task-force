# Napkin math for slides

$$
\frac{n \cdot l}{t} = m
$$

$$
t = \text{Average time to label a single frame (suggested unit: seconds per frame)}
$$
$$
n = \text{Number of labelers}
$$
$$
l = \text{Length of time each labeler must spend annotating frames}
$$
$$
m = \text{Total number of labeled frames}
$$


## Example

Let

$$
t = 5 \ \frac{\text{s}}{\text{frames}}
$$
$$
n = 10
$$
$$
m = 1,000,000 \ \text{frames}
$$

Then solving for $l$,

$$
l = \frac{t \cdot m}{n} = \frac{1,000,000 \ \text{frames} \cdot 5 \ \frac{\text{s}}{\text{frames}}}{10}
= \frac{5,000,000 \ \text{s}}{10} \cdot \frac{1 \ \text{min}}{60 \ \text{s}} \cdot \frac{1 \ \text{h}}{60 \ \text{min}}
\approx 138.88 \ \text{h}
$$

For some perspective, if you organized a 1-hour session to sit down and do nothing but label frames at this rate, you would only need one such session every other day.

If any of these individuals did a solid 8-hour work day doing nothing but labeling, it would be ~17 work days out of the year to reach this.

Or, if you gathered a larger amount (such as 138) of qualified personnel for a single hour, you could clear through it in one go.
